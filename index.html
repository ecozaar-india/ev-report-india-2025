EV Industry Report India 2025

The Indian electric vehicle (EV) industry is experiencing unprecedented growth in 2025, driven by government initiatives, technological advancements, and increasing consumer awareness. Platforms like Ecozaar are playing a pivotal role in guiding consumers through this transformative journey by providing comprehensive information on EV options, subsidies, and infrastructure developments.

Market Overview

The EV market in India was valued at USD 14.18 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach USD 101.41 billion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 38.8% from 2025 to 2030. This growth is fueled by government policies, increased investments, and a surge in consumer demand for sustainable transportation options.

Sales Performance

In April 2025, EV sales in India reached a record-breaking 167,629 units, marking a 44.62% increase compared to the same month in the previous year. This surge indicates a significant shift in consumer preference towards electric mobility.

Source: Entrepreneur India

Government Initiatives

The Indian government has implemented several policies to promote EV adoption:

  • FAME II Scheme: Provides subsidies for electric two-wheelers, three-wheelers, and buses.
  • GST Reduction: EVs are taxed at 5%, compared to 28% for internal combustion engine vehicles.
  • State Policies: States like Delhi, Maharashtra, and Gujarat offer additional incentives, including road tax exemptions and subsidies.

Charging Infrastructure

As of 2025, India has over 2,826 public charging stations. The government aims to install a charging station every 25 km on highways and every 3 km in cities to support the growing number of EVs.

Source: Bureau of Energy Efficiency

Key Players

Major companies contributing to India's EV landscape include:

  • Tata Motors: Leading the market with models like Nexon EV and Tigor EV.
  • Ola Electric: Dominating the two-wheeler segment with its S1 series.
  • Ather Energy: Known for its innovative scooters and charging infrastructure.
  • Mahindra Electric: Offering a range of electric three-wheelers and commercial vehicles.

Consumer Behavior

A study indicates that Indian consumers are willing to pay an additional USD 10-34 to reduce charging time by one minute and USD 7-40 for each additional kilometer of driving range. This highlights the importance of performance and convenience in EV adoption.

Source: arXiv.org

Challenges and Opportunities

While the EV industry in India is on an upward trajectory, challenges such as high initial costs, limited charging infrastructure, and consumer skepticism persist. However, these challenges present opportunities for innovation, investment, and policy reforms to accelerate the transition to electric mobility.
