EV Industry Report India 2025

The electric vehicle (EV) landscape in India is undergoing a transformative shift in 2025. With a surge in consumer interest, supportive government policies, and technological advancements, the EV sector is poised for significant growth. Platforms like Ecozaar are playing a pivotal role in guiding consumers through this evolving market.

Market Overview

As of 2025, the Indian EV market is valued at approximately USD 14.18 billion and is projected to reach USD 101.41 billion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 38.8% from 2025 to 2030. This growth is driven by increasing environmental awareness, rising fuel prices, and government incentives.

Source: Grand View Research

Sales Performance

In April 2025, EV sales in India reached 167,629 units, marking a 44.62% increase compared to the same month in the previous year. This uptick reflects growing consumer confidence and the expanding availability of EV models across segments.

Source: Entrepreneur India

Production Insights

The production of battery-powered passenger vehicles is expected to rise by 140.2% year-over-year in 2025, reaching approximately 301,400 units. This accounts for about 6% of the estimated 5.16 million passenger vehicles projected to be produced in India this year.

Source: S&P Global

Government Initiatives

The Indian government continues to bolster the EV sector through initiatives like the Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Hybrid and Electric Vehicles (FAME) scheme. In 2025, under the Prime Minister's e-Drive Scheme, significant allocations have been made for electric buses across major cities:

City Number of E-Buses Allocated
Bengaluru 4,500
Delhi 2,800
Hyderabad 2,000
Ahmedabad 1,000
Surat 600

Source: The Economic Times

Key Industry Players

Several automakers are intensifying their focus on the Indian EV market:

  • Tata Motors: Plans to launch 10 EV models by 2027, aiming for 25% of its total sales to be EVs.
  • Mahindra & Mahindra: Targeting 20%-30% of SUV sales as EVs by 2027.
  • Hyundai Motor India: Plans five new EV models by 2032, with significant investments in charging infrastructure.
  • Maruti Suzuki India: Aiming for six EV models by 2030, with the first expected by late 2024.
  • JSW Group: Investing $4.82 billion in EV and battery manufacturing in Odisha.

Source: Reuters

Challenges and Opportunities

Despite the positive trajectory, the Indian EV market faces challenges such as:

  • Financing Ecosystem: The current EV financing system is opaque and lacks transparency, hindering mass adoption.
  • Charging Infrastructure: While growing, the charging network needs further expansion to meet rising demand.
  • Consumer Awareness: Continued efforts are required to educate consumers about the benefits and feasibility of EVs.

Source: The Economic Times

Conclusion

The EV industry in India is on an accelerated growth path in 2025, with substantial investments, policy support, and increasing consumer acceptance.
